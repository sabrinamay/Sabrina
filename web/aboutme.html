About Me

Hey there, I'm currently a sophmore at The College of New Jersey.

I am a dual citizen and come from a family of Burmese immigrants.

I was raised Theravada Buddhist;
Always practicing loving metta with the upmost effort.
From this, I have been doing UN programs since middleschool.
Educating and teaching parlimentary proceedures for writting bills:
Enviornmental sustainability bills, Trafficking bills, IAEA bills.

Switching majors, I then started working for TCNJs campus Architect.
Where I was introduced to a new niche of science, technology, and design.
They took design from aesthetics to practical and sustainable approaches.

Now, as an IMM major, I am thrilled to see the world of cognition and tech collide.
Ex; the neuro-cognitive approaches to LUMA fusion when editing videos.
I love building robots and understanding how EEG sensors work with Arduino sensors.

I aspire to be like Neri Oxman, Brian Greene, and Neil deGrasse Tyson:
Communicating curiousity through tech, science, and art.

-The Influence of a Friend-
Christine (the girl in the photo above) and I would study calculus in the freshman lounge, where our peers would run in screaming "this is sparta". While they declared war upon another dorm floor, Christine and I would attack the numbers on our pages and bond over our struggle in academics. I felt comfortable enough to tell her my struggles. That was when she opened up about how she had dyslexia and referred me to the Disability Student Services office on campus. After a thorough and exhausting process—I was confirmed to have a reading disability.

At this moment, I realised the value of my education and how it is a privilege to better myself:

I have the power to answer all the questions I have.
I have the power to choose what I do and discover what I love.
Christine not only gave me resources, she gave me courage. This is why I value education and service; it's helping an individual not only by teaching them what to do, but by making the process easier. Inspiring them to get over any obstacle and to communicate different goals and objectives.



How can we help each other better understand the questions and answers that we have?
